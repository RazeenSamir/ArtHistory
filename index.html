— The Age of Enlightenment —

A Period of Light,
Reason, and Form

Between 1650 and 1775, Europe underwent an intellectual revolution. The Age of Enlightenment brought reason to the forefront of thought — reshaping science, philosophy, and the visual arts.

The Louvre collection from this era captures this transformation: the objects made, the materials chosen, and the forms crafted tell a story of a civilization in motion.

Walk with us through 629 objects. Each one a witness to its age.

Research Question

How did object types
and materials shift
during the beginning of
the Age of Enlightenment?

As Europe moved from Baroque grandeur toward Enlightenment rationalism, the Louvre's collection reveals a compelling transition — from heavy religious iconography toward secular portraiture, decorative arts, and scientific instruments. Materials shifted too: from gilded bronze to refined marble, porcelain, and new world materials.

Key Findings

01

Marble Dominated

Over half of all objects were marble — a classical material that embodied Enlightenment ideals of reason, clarity, and ancient precedent.

02

Sculpture Prevailed

Statuettes, statues, and busts comprised nearly half the collection — demonstrating the era's investment in figural representation and portraiture.

03

The 1680s Peak

Production surged under Louis XIV's patronage — a political use of art that the Enlightenment would later question and transform.

04

Late Intimacy

Terracotta rose in the late period, suggesting a shift toward more private, personal, and experimental artistic forms as the Enlightenment matured.
